COVID-19 is still with us, but the once-urgent need for high-volume PCR testing and viral sequencing has subsided. As a result, the Broad Institute wound down its COVID-19 diagnostic testing program on June 30, 2023 and large-scale genomic surveillance on March 31, 2023. The Broad continues to collaborate with public health agencies and to support public health to conduct research and develop technologies to better understand, diagnose, and treat infectious diseases. The latest information on circulating SARS-CoV-2 variants can be found on the CDC’s COVID Data Tracker.

The Broad Institute’s COVID-19 genomic surveillance effort

With support from the CDC, the Broad launched an effort to perform large-scale sequencing of SARS-CoV-2 for genomic surveillance and public health response in the Northeast. The Viral Genomics Group and Genomics Platform worked together to sequence the virus from thousands of COVID-19 positive samples each week from the institute’s diagnostics lab, with bioinformatics support from the Data Sciences Platform. The results were shared with public health partners in real-time to help slow the spread of concerning variants, and with the scientific community to learn more about the evolution and spread of SARS-CoV-2.
Learn more in a Broad news story and video.

Terra Workspaces

Data and analytical tools are shared openly in the COVID-19 Broad Viral NGS workspace in Terra, so other research and public health labs can explore these resources for their own work. The workspace is designed to enable researchers to go all the way from raw reads to phylogenetic trees using their own data. This workspace is just one of many workspaces for public health genomics.
